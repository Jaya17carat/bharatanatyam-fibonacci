The Concept

Mathematics Encoded in Motion

Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest classical dance forms of India, rooted in the Natya Shastra — a 2,000-year-old treatise on the arts. Its geometry is precise: the Aramandi (half-sit) creates a perfect diamond, mudras fold fingers into sacred angles, and adavu sequences build rhythmic patterns that echo mathematical progressions.

The Fibonacci sequence (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21…) converges toward φ (phi), the golden ratio ≈ 1.618. This ratio appears throughout nature — in sunflower spirals, nautilus shells, galaxy arms. This project maps each category of Bharatanatyam movement to a Fibonacci number, revealing how classical dance geometry aligns with the golden spiral.
